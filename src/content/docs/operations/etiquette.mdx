Automating your workflow is powerful, but with great power comes great responsibility. Isekai Core is designed to be a "good citizen" of the DeviantArt ecosystem, but it relies on you to configure it correctly.

## Avoiding Rate Limits (Error 429)

DeviantArt protects its servers by limiting how many requests you can make in a short period. If you see `429 Too Many Requests`, it means you are moving too fast.

### The "Content" Limit

<Aside type="caution">
Unlike standard API calls (like browsing), uploading files (`stash/submit` and `stash/publish`) has much stricter limits. Posting too many deviations instantly can trigger a temporary ban on your IP or Access Token.
</Aside>

### Recommended Tuning for Bulk Uploaders

If you plan to upload dozens of images at once, you should slow down the publisher. Fast uploads are not necessary for background jobsâ€”reliability is more important than speed.

Edit these values in your `.env` file:

```bash
# Reduce concurrency to 1 or 2.
# Default is 5, which might be too aggressive for bulk uploads.
PUBLISHER_CONCURRENCY=1

# Increase the delay between API calls.
# Default is 3000ms (3s). 5000ms-10000ms (5-10s) is much safer.
RATE_LIMITER_BASE_DELAY_MS=5000

# Add jitter to make behavior look less robotic.
RATE_LIMITER_JITTER_PERCENT=30
```

### The Circuit Breaker

Isekai Core includes a built-in Circuit Breaker. If it detects multiple 429 errors:

- It stops all publishing jobs immediately.
- It enters an "Open" state for 5 minutes (default `CIRCUIT_BREAKER_OPEN_DURATION_MS`).
- Jobs will fail with "Circuit Open" message to protect your account from further bans.
- After the cooldown, it tries one job to see if the limit is lifted.

## Scheduling vs. Dumping

Just because you can upload 100 images in 5 minutes doesn't mean you should.

### Why "Dumping" is Bad

- **Algorithm Penalty**: DeviantArt's algorithm (and Watch feeds) may flag rapid-fire uploads as spam.
- **User Fatigue**: Your watchers will be overwhelmed if you flood their inbox with 50 notifications in one minute, leading to unwatches.
- **Ban Risk**: High frequency increases the chance of automated spam detection flagging your account.

### The Isekai Strategy

<Steps>

1. Upload your bulk batch of 20 images to **Drafts**.

2. Select them all.

3. Click **Auto-Schedule**.

4. Let Isekai release them over the next few days (e.g., 1 post every 6 hours).

</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
This maximizes engagement (your art stays at the top of feeds longer) and keeps your account safe.
</Aside>

## Compliance & Tagging

### AI Generation

DeviantArt requires AI-generated content to be tagged properly.

- In **Settings > Default Values**, enable "Mark as AI Generated".
- This ensures the `ai_generated` flag is sent with every API request, keeping you compliant with DA Terms of Service.

### Mature Content

<Aside type="caution">
Always strictly tag mature content. If you are unsure, use the "Strict" setting in the Draft editor.

Failure to tag mature content correctly is a violation of DeviantArt policies and can result in account suspension.
</Aside>
